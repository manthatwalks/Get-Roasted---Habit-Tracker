# Apex Lad

Apex Lad is a published iOS habit accountability app developed by our team from initial concept through launch. The app is live on the iOS App Store and is supported by a public product website.

The product combines habit tracking, personality-driven coaching, push notifications, and photo-based habit verification. Users can submit visual proof of completed habits, which is reviewed through the OpenAI API to help verify completion. Based on the result, the Apex Lad coach delivers personalized feedback that makes the habit-tracking experience more engaging and memorable.

User feedback showed strong engagement with the app's personalized coaching style, especially the completion messages, comedic accountability reminders, and photo-verification experience. These insights are now informing our team's continued product development around personable motivational coaching and monetizable verification-based habit tracking.

## Coach Personality

Apex Lad is designed to feel like a cheeky but motivating accountability coach. The humor is intentionally light, playful, and tied to the user's actual habit progress, making routine check-ins feel more personal than a standard habit tracker.

Example coach messages include:

- "Proper job. That's another win stacked up."
- "Oi, that habit isn't going to finish itself. Get moving."
- "Not bad, lad. Don't let one good day go to your head."
- "Your streak is on the line. No excuses today."
- "That proof checks out. Same energy tomorrow."

## Key Features

- Habit creation with custom photo-verification criteria
- Camera and gallery-based check-ins for daily habit completion
- OpenAI-powered photo review to help verify whether submitted proof matches the habit
- Personalized coach feedback after successful check-ins
- Streak tracking, daily progress, and calendar-based habit history
- Push notification reminders for incomplete habits and at-risk streaks
- Firebase-backed authentication, habit storage, image storage, and serverless backend logic
- Account deletion and privacy policy access from in-app settings

## Tech Stack
